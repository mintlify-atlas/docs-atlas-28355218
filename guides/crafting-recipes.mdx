This guide covers all the essential crafting recipes you'll need to create your elemental wizard gear.

## Spell books

Spell books grant you access to all spells within an elemental school.

### Tome of the Aquamancer (Water)

**Shapeless crafting**:
- 1 Kelp
- 1 Prismarine Crystals
- 1 Book
- 1 Lapis Lazuli

<Note>
Kelp can be found in ocean biomes. Prismarine Crystals drop from Guardians or can be found in Ocean Monuments.
</Note>

### Tome of the Terramancer (Earth)

**Shapeless crafting**:
- 1 Pointed Dripstone
- 1 Dripstone Block
- 1 Book
- 1 Lapis Lazuli

<Note>
Dripstone can be found in Dripstone Caves biomes, introduced in Minecraft 1.17+.
</Note>

### Tome of the Aeromancer (Air)

**Shapeless crafting**:
- 1 Phantom Membrane
- 1 Feather
- 1 Book
- 1 Lapis Lazuli

<Note>
Phantom Membranes drop from Phantoms, which spawn after you haven't slept for several nights.
</Note>

## Armor crafting

### Elemental Robes (Tier 1)

The basic Elemental Robes provide +20% spell power to all elements.

**Elemental Essence** (required for robes):
- Crafting recipe available in-game
- Check your recipe book after installing the mod

**Armor pieces**:
- Helmet: Standard helmet pattern with Elemental Essence
- Chestplate: Standard chestplate pattern with Elemental Essence
- Leggings: Standard leggings pattern with Elemental Essence
- Boots: Standard boots pattern with Elemental Essence

### Specialized Armor (Tier 2)

Specialized armor sets provide higher bonuses to specific elements:

#### Kelp Armor (Water)
- Follow standard armor patterns
- Uses Kelp as the primary material
- **Bonuses**: +25% water spell power, +2% haste per piece

#### Dripstone Armor (Earth)
- Follow standard armor patterns
- Uses Dripstone as the primary material
- **Bonuses**: +25% earth spell power, +2% critical chance per piece

#### Wind Armor (Air)
- Follow standard armor patterns
- Uses Phantom Membrane as the primary material
- **Bonuses**: +25% air spell power, +5% critical damage per piece

### Netherite Armor (Tier 3)

Upgrade your specialized armor with Netherite using the Smithing Table:

1. Place your specialized armor piece in the first slot
2. Add a Netherite Ingot in the second slot
3. Apply the upgrade to get Netherite Kelp/Dripstone/Wind armor

**Enhanced bonuses**:
- +30% element-specific spell power
- Increased haste/crit stats
- Netherite durability and knockback resistance

<Warning>
Netherite armor requires specialized armor as a base. You cannot directly craft Netherite elemental armor.
</Warning>

## Weapon crafting

### Wands

Wands are quick-casting weapons that provide spell power bonuses.

**Basic pattern** (varies by tier):
- Tier 0: Wood/Gold + element-specific material
- Tier 1: Iron + element-specific material
- Tier 2: Diamond + element-specific material
- Tier 3: Netherite Ingot + element-specific material

**Element-specific materials**:
- Water: Prismarine Shards/Crystals
- Earth: Dripstone
- Air: Phantom Membrane

### Staffs

Staffs provide higher spell power but have slower attack speed.

**Basic pattern** (similar to wands but using different arrangements):
- Follows staff-specific crafting patterns
- Uses the same element-specific materials as wands
- Requires more materials than wands

### Elemental Staff

A unique staff that provides spell power to ALL elements (+5.0 to water, earth, air, and fire).

**Crafting**:
- Check in-game recipe book for the exact pattern
- Requires materials from all three elemental schools
- One of the most versatile weapons for multi-element builds

<Tip>
The Elemental Staff is perfect for players who want to use spells from multiple schools without switching weapons.
</Tip>

## Special weapons (Mod Integration)

If you have certain mods installed, additional weapons become available:

### Better Nether Integration
- **Nether Ruby Wands & Staffs**: Fire-aligned weapons
- Requires Better Nether mod

### Better End Integration
- **Aeternium Wands & Staffs**: Arcane-aligned weapons
- Requires Better End mod

### Aether Integration
- **Valkyrie Elemental Staff**: Sky-aligned weapon
- Requires Aether mod

### Arsenal Integration
- **The Avatar Staff (Tier 5)**: Ultimate staff with unique passive abilities
- Requires Arsenal (RPG-Series) mod or config enabled
- Passive spell changes based on your highest spell power element

<Note>
Mod-specific items can be disabled in the configuration if you want a vanilla-only experience.
</Note>

## Elemental stones

Elemental stones are consumed when casting certain spells.

### Aqua Stone (Water)
- Found in underwater loot chests
- Can be crafted (check recipe book)

### Terra Stone (Earth)
- Found in cave and mineshaft loot chests
- Can be crafted (check recipe book)

### Wind Stone (Air)
- Found in village and pillager outpost chests
- Can be crafted (check recipe book)

<Tip>
Always keep a stack of your primary element's stones in your inventory. Running out mid-combat can be dangerous!
</Tip>

## Disassembling equipment

You can recover materials from unwanted equipment:

**Smelting or Blasting**:
- Smelt/blast weapons and armor pieces to recover base materials
- Wands yield nuggets (iron, gold)
- Staffs yield more materials due to higher crafting cost
- Armor pieces yield ingots or base materials

**Recovery rates**:
- Tier 0-1: Small nugget/material return
- Tier 2-3: Moderate return
- Tier 4-5: Best return rates
